Roger Federer

The Greatest Tennis Player of All Time

Roger Federer with 2012 Wimbledon Championship Trophy

Here's a timeline of Roger Federer's Life :

  • 1999 : No.1 in Junior World Ranking, ITF Junior World Champion, became Professional tennis player.
  • 2001 : Won Hopman Cup representing Switzerland, first Grand Slam QF at French Open and defeated Pete Sampras in Wimbledon.
  • 2002 : Won his first Master Series event at Hamburg Masters, first time in top 10.
  • 2003 : First Grand Slam singles title at Wimbledon, finishing year at world No. 2.
  • 2004 : Won Australian Open, Wimbledon and US Open, first person to do so after Mats Wilander in 1988. Finished the year as world No. 1.
  • 2005 : Won Wimbledon Championship and US Open along with 4 ATP Master Series 100 titles, won in total 11 singles titles.
  • 2006 : Became first man to reach all 4 Grand Slam Finals since Rod Laver in 1969, Losing only French Open.
  • 2007 : Again reached all Grand Slam Finals only losing French Open, only player in the history to win three majors in three years('04,'06,'07)
  • 2008 : Reached three Grand Slam finals only wiining US Open, won Olympic Gold Medal in doubles. Ended year as world No. 2.
  • 2009 : Defeated Robin Soderling in French Open final to complete career Grand Slam. Won 15th Grand Slam title at Wimbledon to surpass Pete Sampras record.
  • 2010 : Won Australian Open along with Stockholm Open, Swiss Indoors and ATP World Tour Finals. Ended year as world No. 2.
  • 2011 : Voted as the second most trusted and respected person in the world, next to Nelson Mandela, ended year as world No. 3.
  • 2012 : Won Wimbledon and Silver Medal in London Olympics, ended year as world No. 1.
  • 2014 : Won Davis Cup for Switzerland for the first time in its history
  • 2015 : Won Brisbane International to become only the third man to win 1000 matches in Open Era after Jimmy Connors and Ivan Lendl, became first man in Open Era to win at least one title in each of 15 consecutive years.
  • 2017 : Won Australian Open and became the oldest player to win Wimbledon, became the second man in the Open era to win Wimbledon without dropping a set after Björn Borg in 1976.
  • 2018 : Won Hopman Cup and Australian Open, became the oldest ATP world No. 1 at 36 years and 195 days of age.
  • 2019 : Still going strong with all the injuries and lockdown due to Coronavirus
